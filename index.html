This workout routine is a combination of cardio and strength training exercises. The first part of the routine focuses on jump rope or jumping jacks for 10 minutes to warm up, followed by a series of rotations and expansions for various body parts including the shoulders, arms, torso, neck, and hips.

The workout section includes exercises for various muscle groups, such as squats, pull ups, handstand push ups, leg raises, push ups, chin ups, dips, and planks. These exercises will help to build strength and improve overall fitness.

The stretching portion of the routine includes a variety of stretches for the neck, arms, shoulders, core, chest, legs, and back. These stretches will help to increase flexibility, reduce muscle soreness, and improve range of motion. It's important to stretch after each workout to help prevent injury and improve recovery.

Overall, this workout routine provides a well-rounded fitness program that can help to improve your overall health and fitness levels. However, it's always important to check with a doctor before starting a new exercise routine, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions.

Breathing Exercise

+ or less 11m
The Wim Hof Method is a breathing exercise technique developed by Dutch extreme athlete Wim Hof. It is a combination of breathing techniques, cold exposure therapy, and mindfulness meditation, aimed at increasing one's physical and mental resilience, as well as their overall well-being. The breathing exercises are meant to stimulate the autonomic nervous system, improve circulation and boost energy levels. The practice involves controlled breathing, where one inhales deeply and exhales fully, repeating the process several times in quick succession. Here's a step-by-step guide to performing the Wim Hof Breathing Exercise:

  • Find a quiet, comfortable place where you can sit down undisturbed for about 10-15 minutes.
  • Take off any tight clothing and remove any distractions (phone, music, etc.)
  • Start by taking 30 deep breaths in quick succession, inhaling deeply through your nose and exhaling fully through your mouth.
  • After the 30 breaths, hold your breath for as long as you can (or until you start to feel uncomfortable)
  • Exhale fully and take a deep breath, then exhale again, allowing your body to empty of air.
  • Hold your breath for another 15 seconds.
  • Inhale deeply, filling your lungs to capacity, then exhale fully and repeat this step three times.
  • Finish the exercise by taking deep breaths and letting your body relax.
  • Repeat this entire process three times, and you'll have completed one Wim Hof Breathing Exercise session.
